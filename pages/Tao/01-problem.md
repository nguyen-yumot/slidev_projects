# The Problem

A decades-old idea — Decision Support Systems — meeting a new generation of autonomous AI.

---

# What is DSS?

**Decision Support System (DSS)** — an information system that supports organizational decision-making.

<div class="pt-6 grid grid-cols-2 gap-6">

<FeatureCard title="Built for ambiguity" icon="i-carbon-help">
DSS targets the cases where no fixed procedure produces the answer — <strong>semi-structured</strong> and <strong>unstructured</strong> problems.
</FeatureCard>

<FeatureCard title="A classic IS concept" icon="i-carbon-book">
DSS has been part of information systems for decades. The thesis asks what happens when <strong>agentic AI</strong> enters it.
</FeatureCard>

</div>

---

# Why traditional DSS falls short

A traditional DSS is fundamentally **reactive and rule-bound**.

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-4 pt-4">

<FeatureCard title="Passive" icon="i-carbon-pause" tone="warning">
Waits to be asked. Never acts on its own.
</FeatureCard>

<FeatureCard title="Static" icon="i-carbon-locked" tone="warning">
Fixed rules that don't adapt to new situations.
</FeatureCard>

<FeatureCard title="Shallow reasoning" icon="i-carbon-chart-line" tone="warning">
Can't plan or reason across multiple steps.
</FeatureCard>

<FeatureCard title="Isolated" icon="i-carbon-data-unreal" tone="warning">
Siloed from the other systems and data it needs.
</FeatureCard>

</div>

<div class="pt-4">

<Callout type="tip">
These four gaps are exactly what an <strong>agentic</strong> approach is meant to close — one capability per gap, slide by slide.
</Callout>

</div>
